# Ticket Lifecycle and Prioritisation

## What Is A Ticket?

A ticket is a record of an incident, problem, service request, or change request logged within a ticketing system.

Examples:

- Password reset
- Outlook issue
- VPN problem
- New starter setup
- Software installation

Tickets help IT teams track work, document actions, and ensure issues are resolved.

---

## Common Ticketing Systems

Examples include:

- HaloPSA
- ServiceNow
- Jira Service Management
- ConnectWise
- Autotask
- Freshservice

Although the platforms differ, the workflow is generally the same.

---

## Ticket Lifecycle

### 1. Ticket Logged

A user reports an issue via:

- Phone
- Email
- Portal
- Chat

Example:

> "I cannot access Outlook."

---

### 2. Categorisation

The ticket is assigned to a category.

Examples:

- Email
- Hardware
- Networking
- Microsoft 365
- Account Access

---

### 3. Prioritisation

Priority is determined by:

- Business impact
- Urgency
- Number of users affected

---

### 4. Investigation

Gather information:

- What is happening?
- When did it start?
- Who is affected?
- What error messages are shown?

---

### 5. Troubleshooting

Perform appropriate checks and attempt resolution.

Examples:

- Password reset
- Restart application
- Verify permissions
- Test connectivity

---

### 6. Escalation

Escalate if:

- Administrative access is required
- Issue exceeds 1st line capability
- Multiple users are affected
- Infrastructure issues are suspected

Always include clear notes when escalating.

---

### 7. Resolution

Once resolved:

- Confirm with user
- Verify service restoration
- Document actions taken

---

### 8. Closure

Update the ticket with:

- Root cause (if known)
- Resolution
- Relevant notes

Close the ticket.

---

## Incident vs Service Request

### Incident

Something is broken.

Examples:

- Outlook not working
- VPN disconnected
- User cannot log in

Goal:

Restore normal service.

---

### Service Request

A user needs something.

Examples:

- New software installation
- New monitor
- Access request

Goal:

Fulfil the request.

---

## What Is An SLA?

SLA = Service Level Agreement

An SLA defines target response and resolution times.

Example:

- Respond within 1 hour
- Resolve within 8 hours

Support teams aim to meet SLA targets.

---

## Prioritisation

Priority should be based on:

### Impact

How many users are affected?

### Urgency

How quickly must the issue be resolved?

---

## Example Priorities

### P1 - Critical

Examples:

- Entire office internet outage
- Company-wide email outage
- Major business service unavailable

---

### P2 - High

Examples:

- CEO unable to access Teams
- Critical user unable to work

---

### P3 - Medium

Examples:

- New starter setup
- Single user application issue

---

### P4 - Low

Examples:

- Software installation
- General requests
- Non-urgent changes

---

## Ticket Ownership

Good service desk engineers:

- Take ownership
- Keep users informed
- Provide updates
- Document actions clearly

Even when escalating, ownership remains important.

---

## Interview Scenario

### Question

Three tickets arrive at the same time:

1. Entire office internet is down.
2. CEO cannot access Teams.
3. User requests Adobe Reader installation.

### Good Answer

> I would prioritise based on business impact and urgency. The office internet outage would be first because it affects multiple users and business operations. The CEO Teams issue would be second due to its importance and urgency. The Adobe Reader installation would be third because it is a routine service request.

---

## Interview Summary

A strong 1st line explanation:

> A ticket records an incident or request from a user. The ticket lifecycle involves logging, categorising, prioritising, troubleshooting, escalating where necessary, resolving the issue, and documenting the outcome. Priority should always be based on business impact and urgency rather than the order tickets are received.
